About this Project

This map was produced using the text from the three voyages of Martin Frobisher in search of the Northwest Passage, 1576-1578. Searching for China and the East Indies on behalf of England, yet unable to pass through the ice of the Hudson Straight, he instead travelled three times to a large sound of Baffin Island, which now bears his name.

The text used can be found on Project Gutenberg and the Internet Archive.

This site is a work in progress, and future developments will include further extracts from the Hakluyt anthologies, as well as integrating the text onto the map. A timeline function is also in progress, but historical data and time descriptions make it difficult to work with presently available tools.

The project files can be found on Github, with the data and xml documents included.

The secondary sources used to locate the places were Wikipedia and this issue of the Royal Geographic Society, which contains a detailed account of the voyages and their locations.

Current issues include multiple mentions of the same place names, multiple unique names for the same location and ambiguous names (i.e. Is the Yarmouth mentioned the port on the Isle of Wight or the coast of Norfolk?). The default projection of Mercator distorts the Arctic region and makes the polar latitudes of Frobisher's voyages appear inaccurate. Additionally, because of the historic nature of the text, often times there are mentions of imaginary places such as Friseland, which is often mistaken sightings of Greenland or Iceland.

The map has been built by UCL Digital Humanities MA/MSc student, Rachel Yales as a demonstration piece for partial fulfillment of the Dissertation aspect of the programme.
