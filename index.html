Marronage & the Prisons of Saint-Domingue

 

This site is home to my undergraduate thesis, which was completed at Michigan State University under the direction of some wonderful mentors in the History Department and at MATRIX Center for Digital Humanities. Feel free to browse around and hopefully content will be added continuously. Use the top menu bar to navigate.

Project Summary

Basically, this project involves databasing thousands of prison lists describing runaway slaves in 18th century Saint-Domingue. It also attempted to create an accurate historical geography for the colony using GIS technology. The database and GIS were then united with scholarly research to analyze and draw conclusions about marronage and geography in Saint-Domingue.

Bio

My name is Rachel Yales and I am a history undergrad student at Michigan State University. I wanted to finish off my undergrad career with something digital, and so I decided to make my senior thesis project online. In addition to my work here, I've also been planning some epic post-grad travels.
